Placement Process

A systematic approach to connecting students with opportunities

Our comprehensive placement process ensures transparency, fairness, and maximum opportunities for every student. From initial registration to final offer acceptance, we provide guidance and support at every step.

Step-by-Step Process

Detailed breakdown of our placement procedure

1

Student Registration

Students must register through the placement portal by providing accurate academic credentials, including CGPA, course details, and personal information. All documents must be verified and uploaded.

  • Complete online registration form with accurate personal and academic details
  • Upload academic transcripts (all semesters) in PDF format
  • Submit professionally formatted resume and cover letter (if required)
  • Verify and update contact information (email, phone, address)
  • Upload passport-size photograph and identity proof
  • Review and confirm all submitted information

üí° Tip: Double-check all information before submission. Inaccurate data may delay or disqualify your application.

2

Eligibility Screening

The placement cell reviews all registrations against eligibility criteria set by recruiting organizations. Students meeting minimum requirements are shortlisted for further rounds.

  • Academic performance verification against minimum CGPA requirements
  • Eligibility criteria matching (branch, year, backlogs, etc.)
  • Document authenticity check and validation
  • Shortlist generation based on company-specific requirements
  • Notification of eligibility status via email and portal

üí° Tip: Maintain a good academic record. Most companies require minimum 60-70% CGPA with no active backlogs.

3

Pre-Placement Talk

Recruiting organizations conduct pre-placement talks to introduce their company, roles, compensation packages, and career growth opportunities to shortlisted students.

  • Company presentation covering culture, values, and growth opportunities
  • Role and responsibility overview for available positions
  • Compensation package details and benefits
  • Interactive Q&A session with company representatives
  • Application submission deadline and process

üí° Tip: Attend all PPTs for companies you're interested in. Ask thoughtful questions to demonstrate your interest.

4

Aptitude & Technical Tests

Students undergo written tests including aptitude, technical knowledge, and domain-specific assessments as per the requirements of the recruiting organization.

  • Aptitude test covering quantitative, logical, and verbal reasoning
  • Technical assessment based on your specialization and role requirements
  • Domain-specific questions related to your field of study
  • Coding challenges (for technical roles)
  • Result announcement and shortlist for next round

üí° Tip: Practice regularly on coding platforms and aptitude tests. Time management is crucial during tests.

5

Group Discussion / Technical Interview

Qualified candidates participate in group discussions or technical interviews to assess communication skills, problem-solving abilities, and technical expertise.

  • Group discussion rounds on current affairs, case studies, or technical topics
  • Technical interview focusing on core subjects and practical knowledge
  • Problem-solving assessment with real-world scenarios
  • Communication skills and teamwork evaluation
  • Presentation skills (for some roles)

üí° Tip: Be confident, articulate, and respectful. Practice mock GDs and technical interviews beforehand.

6

HR Interview

Final round HR interviews assess cultural fit, career goals, and overall suitability. Selected candidates receive offer letters with detailed terms and conditions.

  • HR interview assessing cultural fit, career goals, and personality
  • Final evaluation and selection decision
  • Offer letter issuance with detailed terms and conditions
  • Offer acceptance confirmation within specified deadline
  • Documentation and onboarding process initiation

üí° Tip: Read the offer letter carefully. Once accepted, you're committed. Negotiate respectfully if needed before acceptance.
